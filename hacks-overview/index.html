Rewired State organized, and (executive) produced both #followthedata hack days

London

Follow the Data: London, was the first event in the series, taking place at Hub Westminster, on the 4th & 5th May, 2013.

10 developers were invited, who formed into three teams. Each team collaborated on building a prototype app.

London Challenges

  1. Flow the Money from Aid to Poverty Reduction
    • Audience: Global bodies, international transparency, and accountability charities and UKAID, DFID, companies, investors.
    • Tools to help people follow the money, and identify links between extractives and poverty reduction (one argument is that taxes from resource rich countries should be used to reduce the need for aid).
  2. Discrepancies and Corruption
    • Audience: Auditors,Global bodies,international transparency and accountability charities and UKAID, DFID, Companies, Investors.
    • Identify discrepancies, in companies and governments for example revenues paid by companies and revenues received by governments, and companies, tracing aid flows and extractives money coming into the countries, where does the aid money end up.
  3. Wildcard
    • What other useful ways can the data be used in the transparency and extractives space? Other potential users to think about are auditors, finance ministers, companies, (socially responsible) investors.

Lagos

Follow the Data: Lagos, was the second event in the series, taking place at the Co-Creation Hub in Lagos, on the 13th & 14th May, 2013.

Five teams of six or so developers, designers, domain-knowledge experts, and representatives of civil society teamed up to address challenges faced by citizens and organizations in the resource-rich nation.

Lagos Challenges

  1. What stories can be told with extractives data, and the people who are affected by decisions made by companies and governments?
  2. Is it possible to identify the effect of extractive activity on the local people (e.g. via improved roads, electricity, health care, public services)?
  3. Is it possible to ‘follow the money’ from oil, gas and mining through government accounts?
